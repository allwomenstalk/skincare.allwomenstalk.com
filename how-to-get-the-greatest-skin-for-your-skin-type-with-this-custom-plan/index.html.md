# How to Get the Greatest Skin for Your Skin Type with This Custom Plan ...

A **skincare routine for your skin type** is really important because it addresses the issues you may be having and enhances what's good about your complexion. In fact, understanding your skin type is the single best way to take the best care of your skin by choosing products designed for your needs. You wouldn't want to use something on your face that's for dry skin if you have oily skin because you would only be making the situation worse. Developing a skincare routine for your skin type means always looking and feeling your absolute best. Using this guide, you should have no trouble figuring out what you've been doing wrong or right so that you have the perfect skincare routine for your skin type. Get ready to turn some heads wherever you go, thanks to NotJessFashion.com and the fabulous tips shared with us.

## 1. Normal Skin

![eyebrow, lip, beauty, cheek, chin,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ly/hv/vn1ub2w8595e535ff2ac4963180576_600x600.jpg)

We all wish we had normal skin, but the truth is, we’re not all so lucky. If you are blessed with normal skin, this is the skincare regimen you should follow. For daytime, cleanse with a toner, but avoid alcohol-based ingredients as they dry out the suppleness of normal skin. Gentle cleansers are good to use, too. Apply a moisturizer that has plenty of SPF, but remember, it’s not about the strength of the SPF, but the generous application; go for two layers. At night, the cleansing routine for normal skin will be the same as during the day. For nighttime moisture, find something with vitamin C, antioxidants, and revitalizing essential oils. Go for a cream or serum to keep that normal skin glowing.

## 2. Dry Skin

![beauty, lip, black hair, chin, long hair,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/vl/p5/dw40xkmn595e5376a6bce503821839_600x600.jpg)

If you have dry skin it means you have cracks on the surface that aren’t necessarily visible to the naked eye. Through these cracks, moisture escapes. So your primary focus is to seal in the moisturizer whatever the cost. This skincare regimen will keep your skin bouncy and shiny without too much effort.

Use a cleaning lotion every day, one that doesn’t foam or lather is ideal, and the perfect cleanser would not have a milk base. No matter what, do not buy or apply any products that include SD alcohol 40, denatured alcohol, ethanol or isopropyl alcohol or you will be asking to dry out your skin like never before. A daytime serum that has vitamin E plus sunscreen will be your best friend. At night, stay true to the daytime cleansing routine and don’t skip it. To get the best out of your sleeping hours, go for a serum over a cream because the molecules are smaller and able to permeate the skin easier. Sweet almond, jojoba, and cranberry oils all have immense powers by imitating skin’s natural lipids and sealing in moisture like never before; apply them directly or add a few drops to your favorite moisturizer. Select eye creams that have peptides to further ensure moisturizing madness that will help you achieve hydrated skin.

## 3. Oily Skin

![eyebrow, lip, cheek, beauty, lipstick,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/o9/f3/djmpn3w1595e53b4d2958981656517_600x600.jpg)

It can be tough managing oily skin. Woman with oily skin have higher levels of sebum production and larger oil glands. This is entirely genetic. While it’s not a bad skin type to have, if you are a makeup wearer or suffer from acne, it can be problematic. Follow this skincare routine for optimal results.

For daytime, cleansing three times a day will help keep the oil production low and keep bacteria at bay which can be an issue for this skin type. Try cleansers that have ingredients like geranium or witch hazel, which naturally cut the oil production without over drying. Do not skip moisturizing, that’s a misconception. Go for oil-free moisturizers with sodium PCA and glycerin in the ingredients because they are humectants that will help your skin retain moisture without all the greasiness. Also, zinc oxide sunsreen is the best choice because it will give a matte finish to your skin complexion. At night use the same cleansing routine as for the daytime. Apply retinol, as it will reduce the size of the pores, as will moisturizers with alpha-hydroxy acides or beta-hydroxy acids. If you’re acne-prone, ingredients like salicylic acid and tea tree oil will keep your skin clear and brighten any dullness.

## 4. Combination Skin

![eyebrow, beauty, blond, human hair color, lip,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wh/mj/fls4l84h595e53d97d67d943148428_600x600.jpg)

Here is where many of us fall in the skin type category. Instead of following two skincare regimes, you want to find a balance.

For day, the skincare cleansing regimen for oily skin is best suited for combination skin, but should your skin become dry, swap out the cleanser for a cleansing lotion. The toner will follow suit. For a moisturizer, go for lightweight and something with antioxidants to maintain brilliance. At night, don’t stray from the daytime skin routine. For serums and moisturizers, you will be wise to select anything that an oily skin client would use, but in some cases, the dry skin suggestions might be a better option.

## 5. Sensitive Skin

![hair, blond, eyebrow, human hair color, beauty,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/qb/nt/cg592p7o595e545fa3ffa369295363_600x600.jpg)

Sensitive skin is not skin that breaks out easily. Nope, that’s not sensitive skin. It means skin that easily irritates or turns red without much agitation. This type of skin requires delicate applications and products, as well as avoiding anything aggressive. During the day sulfate free cleansers are highly recommended as are toners with chamomile or green tea extracts; these ingredients help soothe the skin and restore it to a good pH balance where inflammation isn’t an issue. All products that have alcohol or synthetic ingredients, even scents or colorings, must be avoided. For sun protection, the best bet is zinc oxide SPF. The skincare regimen for the night is the exact same. Be sure to apply a scent-free moisturizer that has vitamin E to calm any irritations that may have occurred during the day.

## 6. Aging Skin

![eyebrow, beauty, human hair color, chin, black hair,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/o8/xu/ie8i3g88595e542420065987558187_600x600.jpg)

Aging skin doesn’t start at any certain age. When you see skin sagging, wrinkles or dry spots appearing, and a general dryness throughout, you’re in the aging skin range. It can start in your twenties, believe it or not. This skincare regimen is about revitalizing, regaining moisture, and boosting collagen.

During the day, the skincare regimen will be the same as the dry skin type. Avoid AHA and BHA in your products. For nighttime, follow the dry skin regime, but add in a gentle exfoliator to remove dead skin cells before they weigh down the skin and deepen wrinkles. For a nighttime serum, of course, select ingredients like collagen, antioxidants, vitamin C, peptides, and hydraulic acid. It doesn’t hurt to get started too early. Eye creams that offer peptides and fast-absorbing power are ideal. Moisturizers with retinols will help even out wrinkle lines and prevent new ones from forming.
