# 9 Proven Ways to Effectively Manage and Clear Blackheads

Those little black spots that appear in the mirror are frustrating at any age. Blackheads aren't just a teenage problem or a precursor to acne; they are extremely common. You might imagine that if you squeeze a blackhead out, that's the end of the matter. Not so. Getting rid of blackheads requires more than your fingers and a mirror—it requires a consistent [skincare routine](https://skincare.allwomenstalk.com/how-to-build-a-skincare-routine-for-beginners/).

## 1. Salicylic Acid

![Person applying a salicylic acid treatment to their skin](https://img.allw.mn/content/ou/ck/nqb7ircw56f53fb09e1eb874710426_500x375.gif)

One of the best ways to clear blackheads is to invest in a quality salicylic acid treatment. This beta-hydroxy acid (BHA) is a staple for skin health because it encourages gentle cell turnover, unclogging pores from the inside out. For best results, use a product with a BHA once or twice daily, depending on your skin's tolerance. Check out [Paula's Choice](https://www.paulaschoice.com/) for highly-rated BHA exfoliants that are gentle yet effective.

## 2. Exfoliate Regularly

![Exfoliation process showing glowing skin](https://img.allw.mn/content/jz/fy/ozvtgyfl56f53fc62507a812134384_488x269.gif)

Regular exfoliation is essential to prevent blackheads before they start. By removing the buildup of dead skin cells that can trap oil in your pores, you keep your complexion clear. Whether you prefer a chemical exfoliant or a mild scrub, consistency is the key to maintaining clear skin.

## 3. Use a Skin Cleansing Device

![Using a facial cleansing brush for deeper cleaning](https://img.allw.mn/content/l6/yq/vonq8qfw56f53fe70bfee751388022_500x250.gif)

If your budget allows, mechanical facial cleansing brushes are excellent tools in the fight against congestion. These devices provide a deeper clean than manual washing alone, helping to lift away debris from your pores. If you're looking for a reliable option, [Foreo](https://www.foreo.com/) offers innovative, silicone-based devices that are hygienic and effective.

## 4. Remove Your Makeup Properly

Always remove your makeup before bed. Leaving makeup on overnight is a fast track to clogged pores and breakouts. Use a high-quality micellar water or a double-cleansing method to ensure your skin is completely clean before applying your nighttime treatments. If you're struggling with stubborn makeup, check out these [best makeup removers](https://skincare.allwomenstalk.com/the-best-makeup-removers-for-every-skin-type/) for every skin type.
