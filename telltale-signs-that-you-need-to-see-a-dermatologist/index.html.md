# 9 Telltale Signs That You Need to See a Dermatologist ...

Sometimes our skin gives us mixed signals and we don’t always recognize the signs that we need to see a dermatologist. As confusing as it can be, there are definitely times that you want to consult a professional about your skin and not just slather on some moisturizer and call it a night! Check out these 9 telltale signs you need to see a skin doctor and put your mind and skin at ease!

## 1. OTC Products No Longer Work

One of the major signs you need a dermatologist to intervene in your skin care routine is when your tube of acne medication is no longer effective. We might blame the product at first or think that there are other factors affecting the efficacy of the medication. But, you could be in need of stronger, prescription medication or you might need to make some lifestyle changes to help your skin clear up.

## 2. You Have Persistent Symptoms

There are times when we get a nasty rash that just doesn’t want to quit, but if you notice persistent symptoms, it’s time to see a dermatologist. Skin allergies are common and we can develop an allergy practically overnight so it’s totally possible you’re dealing with a benign situation. If you experience stubborn itching and redness, it’s possible you have eczema and should consult an expert for lifestyle modifications.

## 3. Your Moles Are Changing

If you spot any moles changing in color, size or shape, it’s definitely a good reason to see a doctor. I know it can be scary, but a dermatologist is the only one who can examine it and determine whether it’s something to be concerned about or not. Don’t wait to find out, it’s definitely better to be safe than sorry in this case!

## 4. You Have Skin Care Concerns

Another good time to see a dermatologist is if you need some help with stronger treatments for skin care concerns like improving the look of a scar or burn on your face or body. While dermatologists can help treat most types of scars, in some cases you’ll need some more aggressive treatment where you could be referred to a plastic surgeon.

## 5. You Have Severe Breakouts

All acne is not created equal and severe breakouts could be one of the signs you need a dermatologist. If you have a type of inflamed acne lesion like cystic acne or nodules, they’re not only painful but they can cause some serious scarring to your skin so you’ll want to intervene ASAP. Take yourself to the dermatologist right away to get evaluated and get some relief!

## 6. Your Nails Are Changing Color

Dermatologists treat hair, skin and nails so although you might want to run to your manicurist when your nails start acting funky, it’s actually a sign you need a dermatologist. Discolored nails can be harmless and could be a result of wearing dark nail polish, but there are some cases where it can be a sign of fungus, malnutrition or even an immune disorder.

## 7. You Have Dry Patches

Since we’re coming up on fall we can expect some dry and sometimes itchy skin, but if you’re finding moderate patches of dry skin, it’s a good reason to see a doctor. Patches of dry skin can also be harmless, but it can also be a symptom of a skin disorder, reaction to medication or immune disorder. Keep an eye on patches of dry skin and see a doctor if they are increasing or not improving.

## 8. You Think You Might Have Psoriasis

If you think you might have symptoms of psoriasis, it’s definitely time to see a dermatologist. There are different types of psoriasis and it can occur on your skin, nails and scalp. Psoriasis is a chronic condition and it has a wide range of symptoms from sore joints to cracked skin so if you have any suspicions that you might have it, see the doctor right away for treatment and lifestyle modifications for some relief.

## 9. You Want Preventative Care

You don’t always have to wait for signs you need a dermatologist, you can get ahead of the game. You can see a dermatologist about how you can minimize skin damage, aging, get a cancer screening or how you can make changes to your life so you can take steps now to prevent a problem later. Seeing a dermatologist and educating yourself about your skin isn’t a luxury, it’s smart!

Did you relate to any of these signs you need a dermatologist? Dermatologists can help you with a host of skin care concerns so don’t hold back. Don’t let embarrassment or doubt prevent you from getting treatment. Dermatologists can help you with treatment, dealing with symptoms and improve your quality of life!
