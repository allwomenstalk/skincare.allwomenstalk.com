# 9 No\-nonsense Skin Care Tips for Acne Prone Skin ...

Having acne can be stressful and frustrating, but there are some skin care tips for acne prone skin that can make a huge difference. There are ways to reduce acne breakouts and prevent damage to your skin by following some simple yet effective skin care tips. Keep reading to make sure you’re using the right products in the right way and help improve your skin today!

## 1. Keep Your Skin Clean

One of the first skin care tips for acne prone skin, or any every skin type for that matter, is to keep your skin clean. Cleanse your face twice daily with a gentle cleanser made for acne prone skin and steer clear of using facial scrubs too often. Using a scrub might seem like a good idea, but it can actually aggravate your skin and cause your skin to be even more inflamed and even spread bacteria. Gently massage your facial cleanser with your clean fingers and rinse with lukewarm water.

## 2. Look for Certain Ingredients

When you’re shopping for skin care products, don’t forget to look for certain ingredients that work especially well for acne prone or even oily skin. Look for ingredients such as benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, glycolic acid or beta-hydroxy acid, which can help break up dead skin cells and prevent pores from clogging. Always use a small amount first to see how your skin reacts before you try a larger amount.

## 3. Tone It up

Toners tend to be a bit on the controversial side, as some skin care experts view them as an unnecessary product that can potentially irritate skin whereas others feel that it’s an ideal product to get rid of excess oil. Try it for yourself to see if it helps you get rid of extra oil on areas like your chin and T-zone. Again, you want to avoid toners that are harsh, so look for toners that will help balance and refresh your skin, such as Clinique’s Acne Solutions Clarifying Lotion.

## 4. Go Oil-free

While some people think that oily or acne prone skin doesn’t need a moisturizer, all skin types can benefit from it. Many acne treatment products have a drying effect and if oily, acne-prone skin gets too dry, it can cause oil production to go into overdrive. Look for lightweight, oil-free moisturizers that are made for acne prone skin. Some great choices are Vichy Normaderm Triple Action Anti-Acne Hydrating Lotion or Yes To Cucumbers Soothing Sensitive Skin Daily Calming Moisturizer if you have sensitive skin. If you’re using retinoids or Retin-A, don’t forget to grab a moisturizer that contains SPF.

## 5. Mask Your Skin

About once a week apply a facial mask, like clay, that can help minimize large pores and absorb excess oil. Peter Thomas Roth makes a great clay mask that also contains sulfur, which is awesome at deep cleaning pores while soothing your skin and ridding it of any impurities. With continued use, you’ll notice changes in the look and feel of your skin and you’ll gladly make it a weekly routine.

## 6. Go Easy on Makeup

If you get an acne breakout, the first thing you might want to do is to cover it up with concealer and makeup, but you want to let your skin breathe. Packing on makeup that’s too heavy or contains oil can clog your pores and even cause an acne flare-up. When you’re looking for a concealer, stay away from cream-based ones and instead choose one like Murad’s Acne Treatment Concealer that contains salicylic acid that’ll help you conceal and heal.

## 7. Don’t Forget Sunscreen

Applying sunscreen on your face when you have acne prone skin might seem like a recipe for disaster since some formulas tend to clog pores, but these days we have many choices that won’t bother our skin. All skin types need protection from the damaging rays of the sun and WebMD suggests trying a gel formula or an oil-free sunscreen to shield skin from the sun.

## 8. Be Flexible

Oil production tends to fluctuate depending on a number of things, like the weather, hormones or even your mood. WebMD reminds us to be mindful of what causes excess oil production in our skin so we can adapt our regimen according to how our skin reacts. For example, you might not have as much of an issue with overproduction of oil in the winter as you do in the summer, so be sure you adjust your skincare regimen so you’re not drying out or over-moisturizing your skin.

## 9. Be Consistent

Lastly, when it comes to skin care of any type, be consistent. Using a certain acne treatment once or using a certain type of moisturizer or cleanser a few times isn’t a good way to ascertain whether the product is really working or not. Give your product time to work before you move on to something new. Otherwise, you’re wasting product and money!
