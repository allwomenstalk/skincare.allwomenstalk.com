# All You Need to Know to Get Fuller Lips with Lip Fillers Instantly ...

Thanks to Kylie Jenner, full lips are what every girl wants these days. If you weren't born blessed with a ripe, plump kisser, there are solutions that will give you the pout you want. [Lip fillers](https://albanylaser.ca/services/dermal-fillers/) are a safe way to give your lips some volume and create the look you want. Before undergoing a lip filler treatment, it's a good idea to understand what it involves. Here's everything you need to know.

## 1. Purpose of Lip Fillers

As mentioned above, lip fillers are a good choice for plumping your lips and making them look fuller. However, there are other great reasons to consider having a lip filler treatment. As you age, your skin thins, including that on your lips. So, having a lip filler can bring back a youthful appearance by filling in the lines and wrinkles that occur as this thinning happens. Lip fillers also help bring back some of the elasticity to the area.

## 2. How do They Work?

In most cases, the lip filler you'll get involves having an injection of hyaluronic acid put into your lips. This helps bring nutrients to your lips and delivers moisture that plumps them up and makes them visibly softer and smoother.

## 3. Types of Lip Fillers

There are many types of lip fillers on the market, but the two most popular are Juvederm and Restylane. Before undergoing the treatment, be sure you work with your injector to determine which type is best for you and always choose a reputable doctor to perform the lip filler procedure. Never have a lip filler done at a beauty spa or party unless the injector is licensed and can prove his or her experience with the procedure. For more information about Albany Laser, [click here](https://albanylaser.ca/).

## 3. Getting Ready

![lip, cheek, chin, lip gloss, eyebrow,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ov/t7/vf5ku2qw5b285b21a1ee7138141393_600x400.jpg)

Much like with other cosmetic procedures, there is some prep involved in being ready to get a lip filler. Most experts will recommend not taking any aspirin, ibuprofen, fish oil or vitamin E in the week before your procedure. Because these items thin the blood, avoiding them before having a lip filler helps minimize the risk of bruising. Your dermatologist might also suggest forgoing alcohol for the week before the treatment.

## 4. What to Expect

The process itself is pretty basic. A thin needle is used to inject the filler you've chosen into your lips in predetermined places to help give your lips an all over plumper and fuller appearance. A local numbing agent is typically used to help relieve the pain and discomfort associated with the procedure. Afterward, ice is sometimes applied to the area to control swelling. You should avoid cosmetics on your lips for a short time afterward as well. You should notice an immediate improvement with the best results coming as the area heals.

## 5. How Often do I Need It?

[Dermal fillers](https://albanylaser.ca/) much like Botox, aren't permanent and will need to be touched up every so often to keep the results. The typical lip filler will last about 5 to 6 months before it's completely absorbed by your body and needs to be replaced. You should be able to tell by the look and feel of your lips when it's time to make the next appointment.

## 6. Who Shouldn't Use Lip Fillers

Lip fillers aren't for everyone and there are certain people who should avoid them. If your aim is to minimize the space between your top lip and the bottom of your nose, a lip filler might only exaggerate the problem. If you're pregnant or nursing, talk about the safety of lip fillers with your doctor before having the procedure done. Likewise, if you have certain skin conditions, you may not be a good candidate for the procedure.

## 7. Side Effects

Fortunately, for most people, the side effects of lip fillers are pretty minor. The most common thing to expect is swelling and bruising, which should go away in the week following the procedure.
