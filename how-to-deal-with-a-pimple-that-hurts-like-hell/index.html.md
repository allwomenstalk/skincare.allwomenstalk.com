# How to Deal with a Pimple That Hurts like Hell ...

Sometimes you get a pimple that swells so large that it becomes quite painful. You aren’t the only one! It’s not comfortable to have a painful zit and changes are you would do anything to get rid of the pain and the pimple. Luckily, you aren’t stuck toughing it out until the breakout goes away on its own. Here’s how to deal with that painful pimple so that you can focus on the important things.

## 1. Dab a Bit of Hydrocortisone Cream on the Zit

Hydrocortisone is often used to treat inflammatory skin issues like eczema, so it makes sense that it can also relieve swollen pimples. You only need a small dab of 1% hydrocortisone cream to find relief. It will go to work on the pimple and help relieve some of the inflammation, which will make the pimple look better, but also make it a whole lot less painful.

## 2. Figure out the Cause of the Acne

If you keep getting huge, painful pimples, it’s time to make an appointment with your dermatologist. Often there is something going on and by treating that problem, you can often get rid of the acne at the same time. Talk to your doctor about what the best option is for you.

## 3. Try Popping Some Antibiotics

You don’t want to take a steady regimen of antibiotics because resistance can be an issue. However, a course of antibiotics can help get rid of bacteria and relieve an infection that might be the cause of your pain. Of course, antibiotics must be taken only with a prescription from your doctor so you’ll have to make an appointment with your doctor to find the right one for you.

## 4. A Crushed Aspirin Will Give You the Relief You Need

Much like hydrocortisone cream can help relieve inflammation and pain, a crushed aspirin can do the same thing. Many people don’t keep hydrocortisone cream on hand, but you probably have an aspirin or two. Take one of them and crush it up and mix it with a few drops of water to create a paste. Smear the paste on the zit and let it sit for a few minutes. Then rinse and repeat as necessary.

## 5. Drink More Purified Water

Skincare experts say that purified water is a much better choice if you’re having skin issues. If your particular issue is large, painful pimples, you might be surprised to find that switching from tap water to purified water can really help clear things up. You can find purified bottled water at most supermarkets so it’s easy to make this change to your routine.

## 6. Change up Your Diet

A poor diet that is full of fast food, junk food and sugar can cause painful skin issues. By making healthy changes you can also help improve the condition of your skin. You should be eating a wide variety of fruits, veggies, lean protein, low-fat dairy and whole grains. This ensures that you’re getting adequate amounts of the nutrients your skin needs to say smooth and acne-free.

## 7. Apply Ice to the Pimple

Not only can ice reduce the swelling associated with a big painful zit, but it can also numb it a little bit so that you don’t feel the discomfort as harshly. Wrap an ice cube in a cloth and gently press it against the pimple for several minutes at a time. You should notice relief in a short amount of time.
