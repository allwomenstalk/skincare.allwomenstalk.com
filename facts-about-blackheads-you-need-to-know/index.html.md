# 7 Facts about Blackheads You Need to Know ...

As with any other subject, facts about blackheads give you all the information you need to identify and treat them. No girl wants to deal with these nasty little skin blemishes, but the truth is that we all get them once in awhile. Knowing all about blackheads gives you the know how and tools to eliminate them quickly. Use these facts about blackheads to keep your facial skin looking its healthy best no matter what life throws your way.

## 1. They’re Open Hair Follicles

Here’s one of the facts about blackheads you might wish you didn’t know. However, understanding what a blackhead is can help you figure out if that’s what you’re facing or if your blemish is something else. So here goes. A blackhead is an opening in your skin that’s stuffed with debris that includes skin, dirt and oil. Gross, right? Fortunately, getting that junk out isn’t as hard as you might think.

## 2. They Aren’t Always Black

Surprise – not all blackheads are actually black in color. Though most are dark, which is why they get their name, some such blemishes can actually be yellow in color. Blackheads vary in visibility and they might be really noticeable on your face, while they hardly show up at all on your best friend’s skin.

## 3. They Can Turn into a Pimple

Many experts caution people with a blackhead to eliminate it quickly or it could become a pimple. Pimples are often the result of clogged pores so if you have blackheads, chances are you already have clogged pores. Much like pimples, blackheads surge with hormones, so you might notice that they wax and wane throughout your life.

## 4. They’re Usually Caused by Oil

Your face naturally produces a certain amount of oil, called sebum, but if you’re one of the many people who have super oily skin, you’re much more susceptible to blackheads. Much of the debris that goes into forming a blackhead is sebum, so if you have an oily skin type, it pays to treat it carefully to keep blackheads at bay.

## 5. They’ll Come Back

Without proper treatment, blackheads will keep returning. Many of you have probably used nose strips. I love how virtuous I feel after using one and seeing all the yucky stuff I just cleaned out of my pores. However, you really need to follow that up with a proper face cleanser and moisturizer to keep your hair follicles clear of debris that causes blackheads.

## 6. There Are Lots of Remedies

Fortunately, you have plenty of options at your fingertips when it comes to getting rid of your blackheads. This includes over the counter products as well as home remedies that you can mix and match to find the most effective choice for you. Baking soda is one thing that many experts recommend. Use it as an exfoliator to clean out your hair follicles and pores. Look for cleansers and facial creams designed to treat blackheads. If you can’t get rid of your blemishes, talk to your dermatologist about specialized treatment.

## 7. Gentle is Better

To keep blackheads from coming back or forming in the first place, be gentle with your skincare routine. Don’t scrub or buff your face vigorously or you leave your skin raw and prone to damage and blemishes. Use gentle skin products as well to keep it healthy and blemish free, no matter what life throws your way.
