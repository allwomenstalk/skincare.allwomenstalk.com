# Face Wipes 🧻 Vs Washing 🧼 ...

It’s a beauty and hygiene debate that has been raging for as long as any of us can remember: **face wipes versus face washing**. You will find people on both sides of the argument who are committed to doing it one way and only one way, but at the end of the day, the only people you should probably be listening to for a definitive answer on this subject are professionals. Taking everything into consideration, here are some things that expert dermatologists want us to consider when thinking about **face wipes versus face washing**.

## 1. Not the Best Job

The first thing to know is that, scientifically, wipes don’t always do the best job of cleaning your skin. Wipes will certainly get rid of the visible majority of makeup, dirt, and grit from your face, but there will always be a residue left over due to the non-penetrative nature of the wiping action that takes place to use them.

## 2. Wipe Film

Wipes definitely do their job in terms of getting rid of visible product and dirt on your face, but something that they also do is leave their own film or residue behind in its place. The very best brands have a moisturising quality that seeps into the skin, but poor quality wipes might leave your skin feeling sticky or tacky to the touch. This residue can then in turn actually attract further germs. Not ideal!

## 3. Not Equal

Just like all other beauty products, not all face wipes are created equal! There are excellent brands, and then there are poor quality brands, and it is up to you to decide how much you are willing to spend on your daily skincare and beauty regime. Those of you with acne prone and oily skin will fare better using wipes than those with dry skin types, so it us just important to know about the quality of your choice, and the specific type of your own skin.

## 4. No Comparison

Ultimately, dermatologists across the board will agree that whilst face wipes can be a good addition, they should not be a full replacement for good old fashioned face washing with cleanser, water and moisturiser. They are good for when you are on the go, or camping, or working out, but you there is no comparison to proper face washing on a regular basis!
