# 7 Stupendous Skin Care Tips for Dry Skin ...

If you’re looking for some helpful skin care tips for dry skin, I’ve got seven suggestions that I think you’ll find quite useful! All skin types have special considerations when it comes to skin care and having dry skin is no exception. Check out these seven skin care tips for dry skin and treat your skin the way it wants to be treated!

## 1. Don’t Get Caught in Hot Water

One of the top skin care tips for dry skin has to include avoiding hot water! Hot water and dry skin are like water and oil, they just don’t mix! Hot water is pretty much bad for all skin types as it can strip the skin of its natural oils and makes dry skin drier. Whenever you wash your face or takes showers or baths, keep it short and warm.

## 2. Blot Skin

When it comes to dry skin care, you’ve got to be careful to be gentle with it at all times. This means being gentle when you wash your face, use skin care products and when you’re drying off your skin. After you wash your face or get out of the shower or bath, blot your skin gently. Dry skin can be sensitive so you want to avoid rubbing or aggravating your skin!

## 3. Moisturize

The next skin care tip suggests that you keep parched skin well hydrated. This is especially important after you get out of the shower. Try to apply body lotion within three minutes of getting out of the shower or bath so your skin retains the moisture from the water and locks it in. Get in the habit of applying moisturizer after you bathe or shower to keep skin hydrated and soft!

## 4. Avoid Alcohol

You might turn to alcohol to unwind after a long day, but dry skin hates it! Whenever you shop for skin care products, always read labels and avoid using products containing alcohol. Alcohol dries out our skin and I think you’ll be surprised at how many products we use that actually contain it! Look for soaps, deodorant and skin care products that contain only mild ingredients to keep dry skin happy!

## 5. Razor Sharp

If you use a razor to shave, a dry skin care tip is to change your razor blades often. According to the American Academy of Dermatology, using dull razor blades can irritate dry skin. It makes sense; dull razor blades are harder on the skin since you can’t exactly get a close shave. They recommend changing razors every 5-7 shaves to ensure a close and comfortable shave.

## 6. Moisten the Air

Humidifiers are not only great for dry skin care but they're also very helpful for people with dry eyes, colds or the flu, allergies and can help prevent nosebleeds from dry air. Dry air can suck all the moisture out of our skin, and if you’re using a heater it can really drop the moisture level in the air. Going to sleep with a humidifier near your bed means waking up to happy and hydrated skin and lips!

## 7. Supplement Your Skin Care

According to holistic practitioner Dr. Andrew Weil, taking Omega-3 supplements can benefit dry skin, hair and nails within six weeks of regular consumption. Omega-3 fatty acids have been known to help delay aging, regulate oil production, keep skin hydrated and prevent acne so it's definitely worth a try!

These dry skin care tips might seem simple but it’s the little things that can make a big difference in our skin! Having healthy skin no matter what your skin type means paying attention to what you eat, drink, apply to your skin and how you treat it. Be mindful of these things and your hard work will show from the inside out! Do you have any dry skin care tips you’d like to share?
