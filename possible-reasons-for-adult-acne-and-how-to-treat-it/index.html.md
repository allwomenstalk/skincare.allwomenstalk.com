# 7 Possible Reasons for Adult Acne and How to Treat It ...

When suffering from acne during our teenage years, the only hope we hang on is that it is just part of the transitional period and soon it will be over, but it turns out that it is not and there are other reasons for adult acne. And although acne suffering is over for some people when they enter adulthood, others are not so lucky and tend to continue to suffer from this skin problem and never outgrow it. In order to clear things up, here are 7 reasons for adult acne and possible ways to treat it.

## 1. Stress

![Stress](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/06234827_5489_500x750.jpg)

As we grow older, our stress levels tend to increase exponentially which could be one of the main reasons for adult acne. Researchers have found out that in response to high stress levels, our bodies tend to produce more androgens, which stimulate oil glands and hair follicles on the skin, leading to acne.. Therefore in order to fix this problem it is important to keep your stress levels low, by exercising, meditating and taking time for yourself.

## 2. Bad Cosmetics

![Bad Cosmetics](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/06234745_6245_525x700.jpg)

Sometimes some of your favorite beauty products can result in a break out, so if you are suffering from adult acne, check the labels on your skin care products. Make sure that they are non-comedogenic, non-acnegenic, and oil-free. You want to make sure that the products you apply to your skin on daily basis are not prone to increase your acne!

## 3. Cell Phones

![Cell Phones](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/06235506_6824_493x600.jpg)

You might be surprised to see your favorite technology on this list, but believe it or not your phone can be the cause of your most hated acne. Just think about it, your phones are constantly exposed to many surfaces with bacteria, and the warmth from the phone plus the moisture from your face can create the perfect environment for bacteria to thrive and cause acne. In addition constant rubbing of your phone against your face can cause pimples due to friction. Therefore try to give your phone a rest and clean your phone with alcohol!

## 4. Sebum

![Sebum](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/06234559_4088_600x811.jpg)

Another cause of your acne can be sebum which is an oily substance produced by your skin’s sebaceous glands. Sebum tends to clog your pores and attract bacteria, which spells a disaster for your skin. The best way to treat you acne in this case would be to use either cleansers or prescribed medications, which you would have to get from your dermatologist.

## 5. Hair Styling Products

![Hair Styling Products](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/06234628_1934_500x749.jpg)

Just like your cosmetic products can negatively impact your skin, your hair styling products can do the same. Styling products can seep onto your forehead, and trap acne-causing bacteria in your pores. Those pores can become inflamed and turn into blackheads or whiteheads. In addition, bangs can also worsen your skin by bringing it in direct contact with pore clogging hair products. So if you notice acne actively showing on and around your forehead try to limit your hair product use or switch to a less toxic brand.

## 6. Family History

![Family History](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/06234706_7175_500x607.jpg)

Sometimes your acne problems are not because of your wrongdoing, but they come from your family history. So if you are noticing an acne pattern in your family, it is more than likely that this skin problem is in your genes. Even researchers say that some people are more likely to suffer from acne due their genetic predisposition. If that is the case, getting rid of it may be hard but taking care of your skin with the right cleansers and lotions can decrease your suffering.

## 7. Hormones

![Hormones](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/10/06234341_4655_600x800.jpg)

One of the most common causes of acne are fluctuating hormone levels. An imbalance of hormones that women often experience during their menstrual cycle, pregnancy, menopause or beginning stages of birth control use can cause your sebaceous glands to excrete more sebum and we all know what that leads to! The only way to fix your problem is to get your hormones in check by waiting so they can stabilize themselves or seeing your dermatologist.

Acne tends to be an ongoing problem that starts at the beginning of our youth and never seems to leave you alone.
