# How to Get Rid of the Redness  on Your Face ...

Your face may be red for a number of reasons, whether it’s due to acne or some other condition. The reason you have a red face isn’t really as important as figuring out how to get rid of that redness, right? Of course, it’s always a good idea to talk to your doctor about your redness just in case it is a symptom of something serious. In the meantime, use one or all of these tricks for alleviating redness on your face.

## 1. Figure out What’s Causing the Redness in the First Place

Your first step to getting rid of redness is to find out why it’s happening. You may need to see your dermatologist to get a clear picture of what’s going on, but you can also keep a journal. In a notebook, record when your face is red and the conditions surrounding it. This might be what you ate, what your emotions are and what the weather outside is doing. With time, you should see a pattern.

## 2. Slather on an over-the-Counter anti-Inflammatory Cream

This basic step is effective for many people and is easy and affordable, making it a great place to start. All you have to do is gently apply the cream to the affected areas on your face and it should help get rid of the inflammation that leads to redness. If this doesn’t work, read on for more intensive ideas.

## 3. Try a Cold Compress to Make Your Skin Feel Better

Much like anti-inflammatory creams, cold compresses help reduce redness and inflamed skin. This is a super easy idea that you can do anytime and anywhere. Simply wet a washcloth with cool water and lay it over your face for several minutes. If you wanted to, you could also place the washcloth in the refrigerator to make it even cooler.

## 4. Make a Paste of Aspirin and Smear It on Your Face

Aspirin is an anti-inflammatory. You know this because it helps reduce the inflammation and swelling that causes a headache or other pain. The basic method also works for redness on your face. Simply crush the aspirin and mix it with water to create a thick paste. Smear the paste on the red areas on your face. Wait 30 minutes before rinsing the aspirin paste off.

## 5. Try a Small Dab of Aloe Vera Gel on Your Redness

Aloe vera is known as a soothing product and is often recommended for sunburns and other skin irritation. Turns out it can also get rid of redness by soothing the reaction that is causing it. To benefit, simply rub the aloe into the red areas on your face. Experts say that you should see a difference in about 30 minutes.

## 6. Lay a Few Slices of Cucumber on Your Face

Much like aloe, cucumbers have cooling and soothing properties. There’s a reason you always see them as part of the iconic spa scene on television. The combination of those properties and the vitamin C in cucumbers can alleviate redness in just a few minutes. Lay cold cucumber slices on the red spots and allow them to work their magic for several minutes. You should see a difference in no time.

## 7. Use Concealer That Has a Green Tint

Because green and red are on opposite sides of the color wheel, green tinted concealer can counteract redness with ease. Don’t worry – it won’t make your skin look green. Use the concealer to cover your red areas and you’ll be amazed at how quickly you can hide redness.
