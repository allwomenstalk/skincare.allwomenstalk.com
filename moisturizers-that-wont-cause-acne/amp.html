Frequently Asked Questions

Can moisturizers cause acne?
Yes, some moisturizers can clog pores and cause breakouts, so it's important to choose non-comedogenic formulas.
What does 'non-comedogenic' mean?
Non-comedogenic products are designed not to block pores, reducing the chance of acne.
Are oil-based moisturizers bad for acne-prone skin?
Not always; lightweight, non-comedogenic oils can be suitable, but heavy oils may worsen acne.
How can I tell if a moisturizer is good for acne-prone skin?
Look for labels like 'oil-free,' 'non-comedogenic,' and 'for sensitive/acne-prone skin'.
Is it necessary to moisturize if I have oily skin?
Yes, moisturizing helps balance oil production and keeps skin hydrated without causing breakouts.
Can I use a moisturizer if I’m using acne treatments?
Yes, moisturizing is important to prevent dryness and irritation from acne treatments.
Which ingredients should I avoid in acne-prone moisturizers?
Avoid heavy fragrances, alcohol, and comedogenic ingredients like coconut oil if you’re sensitive.
Are gel-based moisturizers better for acne-prone skin?
Often yes, because gel moisturizers are lightweight and absorb quickly without clogging pores.
How often should I apply moisturizer?
Apply moisturizer twice daily—morning and night—for best hydration and skin protection.
Can natural or DIY moisturizers cause acne?
Some natural ingredients can clog pores or irritate skin, so patch test and choose carefully.
