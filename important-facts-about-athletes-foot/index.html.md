# The Most Important Facts You Should Know about Athletes Foot ...

There are a variety of facts about athlete’s foot that you should know about, whether you have had the condition or not. It’s always a good idea to be well educated about things so you can treat and prevent them to the best of your ability should they happen to you. According to the Mayo Clinic, people whose feet reside in moist places for prolonged periods of time are the most at risk of developing athlete’s foot. Does that apply to you? Then you definitely need to keep reading these facts about athlete’s foot so you know exactly what to do.

## 1. Starts between the Toes

Generally, athlete’s foot starts between the toes, but then spreads to other parts of the foot. That’s one of the most important facts about athlete’s foot that you need to know because it allows you to detect when the condition first starts. you can then jump right into treating the problem, which will help alleviate a lot of the pain and discomfort that is associated with athlete’s foot.

## 2. It’s Contagious

Great, right? But, yes, athlete’s foot is highly contagious. You can pick it up from floors, towels and clothing that harbor the fungus that causes the condition. The gym and public pool are two places that require caution so you don’t end up with this nasty issue. Make sure you wear flip flops on public floors so you don’t pick it up.

## 3. Often Recurs

According to the Mayo Clinic, athlete’s foot often recurs. You might successfully get rid of the problem, but it often comes back. That’s why it’s so vital to make sure you follow your doctor’s directions very closely when it comes to treating your case of athlete’s foot. That way you can cut down on the chances that you’ll have to start all over again.

## 4. Produces Lots of Uncomfortable Symptoms

Unfortunately, while athlete’s foot isn’t an inherently dangerous condition, it can be painful and uncomfortable. The National Institute of Health report that the most commons symptoms include itching, burning and cracked skin as well as scaly skin. Again, it starts between your toes, but can spread if you don’t take action to treat it as soon as possible. You might also have to battle ulcers or blisters in severe cases.

## 5. Can Spread to Your Hands

Do not, under any circumstances, pick or touch your athlete’s foot without immediately washing your hands after. That’s because it can spread to your hands. As if it isn’t gross enough on your feet, right? So anytime you apply ointments or give in to the urge to scratch your feet, head to the sink and suds up with soap and warm water right away to prevent having to deal with it on your fingers and hands at the same time.

## 6. Several Treatment Options

For many people, over-the-counter medications can get rid of athlete’s foot while alleviating the symptoms at the same time. However, is some cases, the condition doesn’t respond to this type of treatment. If that’s the case for you, it’s important to talk to your doctor about more aggressive treatment options. Left untreated, athlete’s foot can progress to other parts of your body.

## 7. You Can Prevent It

Good news! You can prevent athlete’s foot. As I said above, wear shoes when you walk around public pools, recreation centers and gyms. If you get sweaty feet, be sure to switch out to clean, dry socks often. When you are at home, go barefoot so your feet can air out and the skin stays dry instead of damp. Avoid sharing shoes and alternate days of wear with your own pairs.
