40 Excellent Tips for Treating Any Degree of Sunburn ...

It’s that time of year when you are naturally spending more time outdoors, which can sometimes lead to a sunburn. When this happens, you want to give yourself some relief while also preventing the damage that too much sun can cause. For that reason, you should be ready to treat your burn as soon as it happens. Better yet, you should be wearing sunscreen on a daily basis to prevent a sunburn. Over time, sun damage can lead to aging and skin cancer so proper sun protection is vital.

By Eliza  · Sep 7, 2025

Sunburn, a painful reminder of excessive sun exposure, can wreak havoc on your skin if not treated promptly and correctly. In this comprehensive guide, we've compiled 40 excellent tips for treating sunburn to help you provide the best care for your skin, no matter the degree of burn you're dealing with. Sunburn isn't just a temporary discomfort; it can have lasting effects on your skin's health and appearance. By following these expert-recommended strategies, you can alleviate pain, prevent complications, and support your skin's healing process.

Importance of Treating Sunburn Correctly:

When it comes to sunburn, proper treatment isn't just about finding immediate relief – it's a crucial step towards maintaining your skin's overall health. Your skin serves as a protective barrier, shielding your body from environmental hazards. Prolonged sun exposure, especially without adequate protection, can lead to harmful consequences. Unprotected exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun damages the DNA in your skin cells, leading to painful inflammation and a red, blistered appearance – the telltale signs of sunburn.

Failing to treat sunburn effectively can have far-reaching implications. Beyond the immediate discomfort, prolonged or severe sunburn increases the risk of skin complications such as premature aging, the formation of dark spots, and even skin cancer. The damage incurred by the skin from excessive UV exposure accumulates over time, emphasizing the necessity of addressing sunburn with utmost care. Moreover, sunburn weakens your skin's natural barrier function, leaving it more susceptible to moisture loss, infection, and further damage. By diligently adhering to the tips for treating sunburn outlined in this guide, you'll not only find relief but also contribute to your skin's resilience and vitality in the long run.

In the following sections, we'll delve into a comprehensive array of 40 tips for treating sunburn, ranging from home remedies to medical interventions. Whether you're facing a mild pinkish hue or a more severe burn with blistering, these strategies are tailored to offer relief, accelerate healing, and safeguard your skin's well-being. Remember, proper sunburn care is not just a matter of immediate comfort – it's an investment in your skin's health for years to come. So, let's embark on this journey of effective sunburn treatment, armed with knowledge and techniques that prioritize your skin's radiance and vitality.

1. Cool Compresses

Gently place a soft cloth soaked in cold water over the sunburned area. The coolness provides instant relief by constricting blood vessels and reducing inflammation. This simple technique helps to numb the pain and soothe the skin, making it an essential initial step in your sunburn treatment regimen.

2. Hydration

Sip on water throughout the day to replenish the moisture your skin has lost due to sun exposure. Proper hydration aids in skin repair, preventing excessive dryness and promoting the healing process. Keeping your body hydrated from within supports overall recovery and helps maintain the skin's natural barrier function.

3. Moisturize

Smooth a generous layer of aloe vera gel over the affected skin to provide a protective barrier and lock in moisture. Aloe vera's natural anti-inflammatory properties ease redness and discomfort while its cooling effect offers much-needed respite to sunburned skin. Regular moisturizing prevents flaking and minimizes the risk of infection, allowing your skin to heal effectively.

4. Avoid Harsh Soaps

Opt for mild, unscented cleansers that cleanse without further irritating your sensitive skin. Harsh soaps can strip away natural oils, exacerbating dryness and discomfort. Choosing a gentle cleanser helps maintain your skin's pH balance and reduces the likelihood of aggravating the burn during your cleansing routine.

5. Pain Relief

Consider taking ibuprofen as it not only reduces pain but also combats inflammation at its source. By targeting the underlying cause of discomfort, ibuprofen provides comprehensive relief, allowing you to move comfortably and facilitating your body's recovery process. This over-the-counter remedy is particularly effective when taken as directed during the initial stages of sunburn.

6. Cool Showers

Step into a lukewarm shower, allowing the water to cascade gently over your sunburned skin. Avoid hot water, which can further inflame the burn, and pat your skin dry with a soft towel. The cool water soothes the heat radiating from your skin and promotes healing, making shower time a comforting part of your sunburn recovery routine.

7. Avoid Tight Clothing

Opt for loose, breathable clothing made from natural fabrics like cotton. Tight clothing can rub against sunburned skin, causing unnecessary friction and irritation. By allowing your skin to breathe and providing ample room for movement, loose clothing aids in preventing discomfort and contributes to the healing process.

8. Stay Indoors

Shield your sunburned skin from further UV exposure by staying indoors during peak sunlight hours. Sunburned skin is highly susceptible to additional damage, and taking a break from the sun allows your body to focus on repairing the existing harm. Embrace indoor activities and rest in a cool environment to support your skin's recovery.

9. No Scrubbing

After a gentle shower, pat your skin dry with a soft towel instead of rubbing vigorously. Rubbing can cause friction and exacerbate irritation, delaying the healing process. By delicately patting your skin, you minimize trauma to the affected areas, ensuring a smoother recovery from your sunburn.

10. Anti-Inflammatory Creams

Consult a medical professional before using hydrocortisone creams, which contain a mild steroid to alleviate redness and inflammation. These creams are particularly effective for reducing swelling and providing relief when applied sparingly to the affected skin. Incorporate them into your treatment plan under medical guidance to ensure safe and effective use.

11. Oatmeal Baths

Create a soothing bath by adding colloidal oatmeal to cool water. Soak in this gentle mixture to alleviate itching and calm inflammation. The natural compounds in oatmeal create a protective layer over your skin, effectively trapping moisture and providing much-needed comfort to sunburned areas.

12. Stay Cool

Position fans strategically in your living spaces to keep the air cool and refreshing. Adequate ventilation prevents excessive sweating, which can further irritate sunburned skin. By creating a comfortable indoor environment, you promote relaxation and aid in your body's natural healing processes.

13. Avoid Sunscreen

While your skin is healing, it's best to avoid applying sunscreen directly onto the sunburned areas. Sunscreen can contain chemicals that may cause discomfort or irritation to sensitive skin. Opt for protective clothing and shade to shield your skin from UV rays during this phase.

14. Vitamin E Oil

Gently massage vitamin E oil onto your sunburned skin to capitalize on its skin-nourishing properties. Vitamin E is renowned for its ability to accelerate wound healing and reduce the appearance of scars. By applying this oil, you provide your skin with essential nutrients that encourage rejuvenation and minimize potential long-term effects of sunburn.

15. Cucumber Slices

Lay cool cucumber slices onto your sunburned skin for a refreshing and calming effect. Cucumbers contain antioxidants and silica, which help reduce swelling and promote skin repair. The hydrating properties of cucumber slices alleviate discomfort, making this a natural remedy that complements your sunburn treatment routine.

16. Stay Away from Heat

Refrain from activities that expose your sunburned skin to heat sources, as this can intensify discomfort. Heat can exacerbate inflammation and hinder the healing process. Opt for cooler activities and environments until your skin has fully recovered to ensure optimal healing.

17. Avoid Exfoliation

Skip using exfoliating products or tools on sunburned skin, as they can worsen irritation. Exfoliation can disrupt the healing process and lead to further redness and discomfort. Prioritize gentle, non-abrasive skincare during your recovery period to support your skin's natural renewal process.

18. Hyaluronic Acid Serum

Apply a hyaluronic acid serum to damp skin after cleansing to lock in moisture and promote hydration. Hyaluronic acid acts as a humectant, attracting and retaining water in the skin, which is essential for healing sunburn. By incorporating this serum into your routine, you support your skin's recovery by ensuring optimal hydration levels.

19. Rest

Allow your body the rest it needs to channel its energy towards healing. Restorative sleep facilitates cellular repair and regeneration, contributing to a faster recovery from sunburn. Prioritize quality sleep and create a relaxing bedtime routine to maximize your body's healing potential.

20. Cold Milk Compress

Immerse a soft cloth in cold milk and gently press it onto your sunburned skin. The cold temperature and proteins in milk work together to create a soothing sensation, reducing redness and calming inflammation. This age-old remedy offers quick relief and is a valuable addition to your sunburn treatment repertoire.

21. Avoid Hot Tubs

Hot tubs can intensify the discomfort of sunburned skin due to the heat. The high temperature can lead to further inflammation and potentially worsen your condition. Opt for cooler activities and environments to minimize the risk of exacerbating your sunburn.

22. DIY Vinegar Spray

Create a mixture of water and white vinegar in a spray bottle and lightly mist it over your sunburned skin. Vinegar's acetic acid content can help balance pH levels and reduce pain and itching. The cooling sensation of the mist provides immediate relief, making this DIY spray a handy tool for managing sunburn discomfort.

23. Anti-Itch Cream

Apply a thin layer of hydrocortisone cream to sunburned areas to alleviate itching and soothe the skin. Hydrocortisone's anti-inflammatory properties help reduce redness and discomfort, promoting a more comfortable healing process. Incorporate this cream into your routine as needed to address the itching that often accompanies sunburn.

24. Fruit Pulp Masks

Gently spread a mixture of mashed fruits like papaya or banana onto your sunburned skin for a natural and revitalizing treatment. These fruits contain enzymes and antioxidants that aid in exfoliation and promote skin regeneration. The soothing and nutrient-rich mask provides a burst of nourishment, aiding in the restoration of sun-damaged skin.

25. Stay Shaded

Seek out shady spots and wear protective clothing, such as wide-brimmed hats and long-sleeved shirts, when venturing outdoors. Shielding your sunburned skin from direct sunlight prevents further damage and allows your body to focus on healing. Embrace the shade and adopt sun-safe practices to support a speedy recovery.

26. Cool Water Soaks

Immerse yourself in a bathtub filled with cool water infused with baking soda to ease discomfort and reduce redness. Baking soda's alkaline properties help balance the skin's pH levels, providing relief from itching and promoting healing. Regular cool water soaks offer a gentle and refreshing way to care for your sunburned skin.

27. Witch Hazel

Apply witch hazel to your sunburned skin using a soft cotton pad. Witch hazel's astringent properties help tighten the skin, reduce inflammation, and provide relief from pain. Its gentle yet effective nature makes it a valuable addition to your sunburn treatment routine, assisting in the healing process while soothing discomfort.

28. Avoid Irritants

Steer clear of scented products, harsh detergents, and perfumed lotions that can further irritate your sunburned skin. These irritants can exacerbate redness and discomfort, hindering the healing process. Opt for fragrance-free, hypoallergenic products to ensure that your skin remains as comfortable as possible during its recovery.

29. Cortisone Cream

Consult a healthcare professional before applying low-potency cortisone cream to sunburned areas. Cortisone cream's anti-inflammatory properties help reduce redness and itching, providing targeted relief. Under medical guidance, incorporate this cream into your routine to effectively manage discomfort and promote healing.

30. Silk Pillowcases

Sleep on silk pillowcases to minimize friction and irritation during the night. Silk's smooth texture reduces rubbing against your sunburned skin, preventing further discomfort. By making this simple switch, you create a conducive environment for overnight healing, enhancing your overall sunburn treatment efforts.

31. Cold Yogurt Pack

Gently spread plain, cold yogurt onto your sunburned skin for an instant cooling effect. Yogurt's lactic acid content exfoliates dead skin cells, while its probiotics soothe inflammation. This kitchen remedy offers a refreshing and nourishing solution, providing relief and supporting your skin's natural recovery process.

32. Chamomile Tea Soak

Brew a pot of chamomile tea, allow it to cool, and add it to your bath for a calming and anti-inflammatory soak. Chamomile's soothing properties help alleviate redness and discomfort, making it an excellent option for easing sunburn symptoms. Indulging in a chamomile-infused bath provides a gentle and relaxing way to care for your sunburned skin.

33. Proper Sun Protection

After your sunburn has healed, prioritize sun protection by applying a broad-spectrum sunscreen with a high SPF before going outside. Shielding your skin from future UV exposure helps prevent further sunburn and minimizes the risk of long-term skin damage. Invest in quality sun protection products and establish sun-safe habits for ongoing skin health.

34. Humidifier

Use a humidifier in indoor spaces to maintain optimal humidity levels and prevent your skin from drying out. Adequate moisture in the air helps prevent excessive skin dryness, supporting the healing process of your sunburn. Incorporating a humidifier into your environment enhances your skin's comfort and accelerates recovery.

35. Consult a Dermatologist

If you experience severe sunburn symptoms, such as intense pain, extensive blistering, or signs of infection, seek guidance from a dermatologist. Professional medical assessment ensures that your sunburn receives appropriate care and minimizes the risk of complications. Consulting a dermatologist offers expert support tailored to your specific condition, promoting effective healing.

36. Aloe Ice Cubes

Create soothing aloe ice cubes by filling an ice cube tray with aloe vera gel and freezing it. Gently glide the cool aloe ice cube over your sunburned skin for targeted relief. The combination of aloe vera's anti-inflammatory properties and the cold temperature of the ice provides a double dose of comfort. This innovative approach offers a refreshing and effective way to manage the discomfort of sunburn while simultaneously promoting healing.

37. Chamomile Tea Soak

Brew a strong pot of chamomile tea, allow it to cool, and pour it into a spray bottle. Mist this calming chamomile-infused concoction onto your sunburned skin for instant relief. Chamomile's anti-inflammatory and antioxidant-rich nature helps soothe redness, reduce irritation, and accelerate healing. This soothing mist serves as a versatile addition to your sunburn care routine, providing both comfort and nourishment to your skin.

38. Proper Sun Protection

After your sunburn has healed, incorporate a high-quality, broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher into your daily routine. Apply it generously to all exposed skin, even on cloudy days, to shield your skin from harmful UV rays. Sunscreen is your frontline defense against future sunburn and skin damage. By consistently practicing sun-safe habits and making sunscreen an integral part of your skincare regimen, you actively safeguard your skin's health and maintain its youthful appearance.

39. Humidifier

Introduce a humidifier to your living spaces, ensuring a consistent level of moisture in the air. Humidifiers are particularly beneficial in dry environments, as they help prevent your sunburned skin from becoming overly dry and flaky. By maintaining optimal humidity levels, you provide your skin with the ideal conditions for healing, minimizing discomfort, and supporting the regeneration of healthy skin cells.

40. Consult a Dermatologist

If your sunburn is accompanied by severe symptoms, persistent pain, widespread blistering, or signs of infection, seek professional guidance from a dermatologist. Dermatologists are trained to assess and treat various skin conditions, ensuring that your sunburn receives the appropriate care. Their expertise allows them to tailor a treatment plan specific to your condition, addressing both immediate concerns and potential long-term effects. Consulting a dermatologist empowers you with comprehensive support, helping you navigate the complexities of sunburn recovery and ensuring your skin's optimal health.

Each of these 40 tips for treating sunburn provides a unique approach to managing and healing sun-damaged skin. By incorporating a combination of these strategies into your routine, you can effectively address the discomfort, inflammation, and potential long-term effects of sunburn. Remember, consistent care and attention are key to supporting your skin's recovery and long-term health.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I soothe mild sunburn quickly?
Apply cool compresses and aloe vera gel to calm the skin and reduce inflammation.
What’s the best moisturizer for sunburned skin?
Use a fragrance-free, hydrating moisturizer with ingredients like aloe or chamomile.
Is it okay to pop blisters caused by sunburn?
No, avoid popping blisters to prevent infection and promote natural healing.
When should I see a doctor for sunburn?
Seek medical help if you experience severe pain, blisters covering large areas, or symptoms like fever and chills.
Can drinking water help with sunburn recovery?
Yes, staying hydrated helps your skin heal by replenishing fluids lost through sun exposure.
Are there any home remedies that actually work for sunburn?
Applying cool milk compresses or cucumber slices can soothe sunburned skin effectively.
Can I use sunscreen on already sunburned skin?
Wait until the skin has cooled and healed before applying sunscreen to prevent further irritation.
Does sunburn affect all skin types the same way?
All skin types can get sunburned, but lighter skin tends to burn faster and more severely.
How long does sunburn usually take to heal?
Most sunburns heal within 3 to 7 days, depending on the severity of the burn.
Can makeup be worn over sunburned skin?
It’s best to avoid makeup until the skin has healed to prevent irritation and allow skin to breathe.
