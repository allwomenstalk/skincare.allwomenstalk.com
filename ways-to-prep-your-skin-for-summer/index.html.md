# Killer Ways to Prep Your Skin for Summer ...

Looking for some **ways to prep your skin for summer** Summertime is time to get outside and bask in the glory of the warming sunshine, but because of this, it’s fair to say that you need to be paying a little more attention to your skincare regime compared to the spring or autumn. You don’t need me to tell you just how damaging prolonged exposure to the sun can be on your face, so if you want to enjoy summer to the max with the least amount of consequences possible, you need to make sure that you are taking care of your skin! Here are some great **ways to prep your skin for summer**.

## 1. Even out Your Skin Tone

This is one of the best **ways to prep your skin for summer**. When the sun is out, you are much more likely to tan, and when you tan the pigmentation differences in your skin are much more accentuated. In order to counterbalance this, try your best to clear up any acne marks and hyperpigmentation on your face so that when you start to tan, those areas will not look darker than the rest of your body.

## 2. Smooth Roughness

The sun can be tough on your skin through prolonged exposure, so you should work to eliminate and smooth any roughness that you have before the full effect of a summer heatwave strikes. If you have a few dollars to spare, then one way to produce a really amazing smoothness to your face is by splashing out for a few laser treatment procedures. They will get the collagen production up in your skin, which is the key to looking smooth.

## 3. Heal Dry Skin from Winter

The main problem with skin in the winter is the fact that it can get really dry and damaged from things like strong winds and dry cold air. As the sun comes out, any skin that is still dry will look flaky and have almost a grey tone, so make sure that you moisturise to the high heavens in preparation of the hotter weather coming back to town.

## 4. Hair Removal

It’s not just the skin on your face that you should be thinking about in this scenario! When bikini season comes around, it’s probably time to start caring for your downstairs area again in the form of some hair removal and maintenance! Don’t leave this until last minute, because things like a wax rash or ingrown hairs from shaving aren’t the best look for the beach!
