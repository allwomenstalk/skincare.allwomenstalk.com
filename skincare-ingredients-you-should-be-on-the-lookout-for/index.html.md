# 7 Skincare Ingredients You Should Be on the Lookout for ...

When you shop for products for your body, there are some skincare ingredients you should be watching for. They are the ones that can help your skin, no matter what the issue and will help keep it looking young and beautiful. If you have specific skincare concerns, such as psoriasis or eczema, it’s a good idea to talk to a doctor about the right products for you. Meanwhile, the rest of us can keep our eyes open for the following skincare ingredients for the best skin possible.

## 1. Ceramide

This is one of the most important skincare ingredients. It works by helping to hold your skin cells together, helping create a barrier to the world. By using products that contain ceramide, you can boost this process while also counteracting the damage that the environment can cause to your skin. It’s available in many products, which makes it easy to find.

## 2. Alpha Lipoic Acid

This ingredient, also called ALA, is a wonder one for your skin. In general, it appears in facial creams and lotions because it has powerful anti-aging properties. It’s an antioxidant that is easily absorbed by the skin. It also works to fight free radical damage to protect your skin from the cellular changes that can lead to all sorts of issues, including skin cancer.

## 3. Neuropeptides

They’re not as scary as they sound. Neuropeptides are an ingredient that has a host of magical powers. They can fight the signs of aging, including combatting wrinkles and fine lines. They also add moisture to the skin, which helps keep it plump and healthy. They also play a role in healthy resilience, skin tone and texture. See – magical, right?

## 4. Retinol

Much like ALA, retinol is an ingredient that is most common in facial creams because it has anti-aging benefits. Retinol works by exfoliating your skin, helping get rid of the old, dead cells and promoting the new ones to come through. It also boosts collagen production, which helps keep your skin looking young and radiant.

## 5. Alpha Hydroxy Acid

Alpha hydroxy acid is not a bad ingredient, despite the presence of acid in the name. This ingredient is derived from natural byproducts and is responsible for counteracting dull skin. By using products that contain it, you can brighten your complexion and give your skin that natural glow you crave.

## 6. Vitamin C

Vitamin C is present in skincare products in several forms. It’s an antioxidant that fights free radical damage so you can use it prevent things like skin cancer and the signs of aging. It’s also an ingredient that can help regenerate vitamin E under the skin’s surface. Without adequate amounts of vitamin E, your skin won’ be healthy in general. So it pays to look for products that contain vitamin C, as well as being sure to eat plenty of foods that contain the nutrient.

## 7. Polyenylphosphotidyl Choline

This ingredient, also called PPC is one that fights dry skin to a great degree. Not only does it moisturize your skin, but it also works to heal chapped skin and can improve the appearance of dry, cracked skin. If that’s not an ingredient you want to seek out, I don’t know what is. Look for it in body creams and facial lotions to provide the benefits to your entire body.

What ingredients do you look for when you buy skincare products? I tend toward those that fight wrinkles.
