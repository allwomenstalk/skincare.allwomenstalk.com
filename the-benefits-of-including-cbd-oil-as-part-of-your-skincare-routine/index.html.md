# The Benefits of Including CBD Oil as Part of Your Skincare Routine ...

Do to recent legalisations across several different US states and countries in the last few years, the presence of CBD oil in products and medicines has seen a steep surge in popularity. If you are unfamiliar with CBD oil, it is essentially an extract of the cannabis plant that can be used in lots of different products to positive and beneficial effect. Its use in medicine and pain relief has been well publicised, but what about cosmetics? Here is a little bit of information to help you decide: Should CBD oil be part of your skincare routine?

## 1. Skin Conditions

Up to 80% of young people suffer from acne, as well as many adults, and CBD oil has been found to be able to reduce the amount of sebum that is produced by your body. This is due to its anti-inflammatory properties, so the less inflamed your skin is, the less sebum is produced, and the less debris there is to clog your pores and hair follicles. [Add some CBD beauty products](https://flawlesscbd.co.uk/collections/cbd-bath-bomb/) into your daily routine to reduce inflammation and boost your skin’s overall health.

## 2. Tattoos

If you are someone who likes to get lots of new tattoos, then CBD oil can be useful for alleviating skin irritation in the healing process. There are lots of creams on the market that can act as pain relief, not only for when you get inked, but also for if you should ever feel the need to have a regrettable one laser removed! The CBD oil in the creams obviously helps to reduce inflammation and pain which are two common consequences of getting a new tattoo.

## 3. Hype

There are certain sections of people who believe that the hype behind CBD oil is far bigger than the actual benefits it can provide. There is no argument that it is a great ingredient that has massive potential, but there are [different opinions](https://lifestyle.allwomenstalk.com/reasons-its-okay-to-have-different-opinions/) in whether the way it is currently being used is the most helpful and useful for consumers. It’s a case of ‘watch this space’!

## 4. Concerns

Coming from cannabis, should you be concerned about using CBD oil? It might seem like a risqué or scary thought if you haven’t had any dealings with cannabis before, but there is nothing to worry about on the legal front. Last year, the government announced that CBD oil was to be approved for use as a medical drug. The skincare sector hasn’t been fully approved yet, but if one side of has been okayed, then the other is surely soon to follow.
