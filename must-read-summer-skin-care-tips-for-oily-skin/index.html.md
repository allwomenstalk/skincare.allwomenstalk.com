# 7 Summer Care Tips for Girls with Oily Skin ...

Dealing with **oily skin** in those hot summer months is like trying to catch a greased pig at a county fair-slippery, frustrating, and frankly a bit of a mess. As someone who has lived through more hot and humid summers than I care to count, I can assure you that these **summer care tips** are not just a bunch of hot air. Welcome to 2024, where we’re here to figure out how to maintain a glowing complexion without looking like we’ve just run a marathon!

The first thing to know about caring for **oily skin** during summertime is that prevention is better than cure. Think of it as an investment strategy but for your face. Regular **cleansing** becomes as crucial as a morning coffee run. Okay, maybe not that crucial (let’s not get crazy here), but it’s pretty important. You don’t want your pores to turn into mini-volcanoes of clogged gunk. And trust me, I say this from personal experience when I didn't heed this advice and ended up looking like a pizza from a 90’s cartoon!

Now, let’s talk about the importance of _hydration_. I know what you’re thinking, "Why on earth would I want to add more moisture to my already **oily skin**?" The answer lies in finding the right **moisturizer**, one that hydrates without turning you into an oil slick. _Water-based moisturizers_ can become your best friend. Just like friends, choose them wisely. This way, you'll avoid those breakouts that seem to pop up as frequently as summer BBQs.

And speaking of breakouts, sunscreen is a non-negotiable. I get it, you’re probably thinking that applying sunscreen might make you feel like you’re wearing a greasy mask. This is where **oil-free sunscreen** comes into play. Not only does it protect your skin from those harmful rays, but it also keeps your face from becoming a slip 'n slide of oil.

One of the often-overlooked tips is maintaining a _healthy diet_. You are what you eat, and if you’re munching on chips and sipping sugary sodas all summer long, don’t be surprised when your face declares mutiny. Lean towards a _balanced diet_ rich in fruits and veggies, because those antioxidants are like little soldiers for your skin, battling off the bad stuff. Dive deeper into this in our section on [diet tips](#section4).

Finally, try to remember that **oily skin** isn’t the enemy. With the right **summer care tips**, you can enjoy everything from beach days to backyard parties without worrying about your complexion. So, slap on that sunscreen, grab a big bottle of water, and let’s tackle this summer with skin that’s not just glistening with sweat but positively glowing with good health!

## 1. Change Cleansers

![hair, human hair color, face, eyebrow, blond,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/g0/dw/r5qlnslx5947cfdd30fd4133361311_600x600.jpg)

One of the lesser known summer skin care tips for oily skin is that you might need an acne-fighting cleanser even if you aren’t prone to breakouts. The rationale behind this is that acne cleansers contain salicylic acid, which helps get rid of excess oil and sebum so you’re less likely to be oily throughout the day. Salicylic acid is also known for helping keep pores clean and gently removing excess skin. If you’d rather DIY it, try making a mask of mashed strawberries (a natural source of salicylic acid) with a squeeze of lemon to brighten and reduce oil.

## 2. Wear Sunscreen

![hair, human hair color, face, eyebrow, black hair,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/it/eg/lhjoi9zf5947cff46b88f860709394_600x600.jpg)

According to Allure, wearing certain types of sunscreen can help keep oil in check. They suggest skipping your regular moisturizer in the morning for a mineral based sunscreen that contains micronized zinc, titanium oxide or denatured alcohol to absorb oil and keep skin matte. Remember to use a teaspoon for your face and a shot glass worth of sunscreen for your body.

## 3. Be Soft and Gentle

![hair, face, eyebrow, black hair, person,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/iv/iu/hiescxj25947d018db13c278119581_600x750.jpg)

Oily skin can be so frustrating at times that it might drive us to scrub our skin really hard or use harsh products on our skin. But that’s not the way to go! All skin types naturally produce oil that’s meant to protect our skin from minor irritations and keep skin hydrated. If we scrub our skin too hard in an attempt to reduce shine, we can actually end up stimulating oil glands. Always be gentle when handling skin and use mild cleansers to keep skin balanced.

## 4. Exfoliate Weekly

![face, hair, person, eyebrow, blue,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/tf/b5/ovfeo2y85947d03270b09827672530_600x750.jpg)

Exfoliating is a very important part of any skin care routine. It’s especially important for oily skin to help control oil production, get rid of dead skin cells, prevent clogged pores and help skin better absorb any other oil-controlling skin care products you might be using. Look for gentle facial exfoliators that won’t over-dry or irritate skin. Oily-skinned ladies can actually exfoliate up to two times per week.

## 5. Forget Foundation

![hair, face, black hair, eyebrow, clothing,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/qi/b2/g3sixr7d5947d050741bb191685073_600x749.jpg)

I don’t know about you, but I’m not too crazy about the idea of wearing a heavy layer of foundation when it’s super hot outside. Some formulas can look and feel heavy, and they can melt into your pores and end up clogging them! Switch to a mineral foundation, which helps soak up oil, or a tinted moisturizer, which forms a barrier over your skin so all of your makeup stays put.

## 6. Blot Instead of Powder

![hair, face, black hair, eyebrow, nose,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/nd/bw/lszqnwbx5947d06b819b0208534878_600x600.jpg)

It’s tempting to want to apply a lot of powder to your face with a powder puff or brush to set makeup and try to keep it matte, but it doesn’t always work. Many times, wearing too much powder can actually make your face look cakey and unnatural. Instead, try using blotting papers that contain a small amount of translucent powder, suggests cosmetic chemist Jim Hammer. With blotting papers, you’re absorbing excess oil without drying out skin and you get just a touch of powder.

## 7. Use Clay Masks

_ Clay-based masks are a must-have for anyone with oily skin. They help control oil production, clean out any impurities and according to Dr. Oz, the effects of a clay mask can last for days! Just be sure you don’t leave it on too long or it can dry out skin. Then, you’ll just end up applying a bunch of moisturizer to counteract it, which can be counterintuitive. Wait for your mask to dry, just not to the point where it’s tight and starting to crack.

Use these simple tips for a radiant complexion all summer long. Having oily skin can be annoying, but there are many ways to tone it down and keep oil levels in check. Do you know of any good summer skin care tips for oily skin?
