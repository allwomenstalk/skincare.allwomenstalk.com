# 10 Common Skin Care Ingredients Decoded ...

Ever get confused looking at endless lists of common skin care ingredients? I’m with you!These days, there are so many different ingredients in skin care that we know nothing about. And if we’re putting it on our faces and bodies, we should know exactly what they are! Take a look at these common skin care ingredients below and be an educated consumer!

## 1. Salicylic Acid

You’ve probably seen this common skin care ingredient on lots of skin care products. This ingredient is often recommended to those who have acne or oily skin because it helps exfoliate and cleanse our faces! Don’t shy away from this ingredient just because it’s an acid, it can help reveal fresh and beautiful skin!

## 2. Hydroquinone

This common skin care ingredient is typically seen in creams that aim to lighten the skin. Hydroquinone was once the It ingredient when it came to fading hyperpigmentation,then it was thought to be a possible carcinogen. This caused some criticism from dermatologists who stopped recommending it. Some still consider it the gold standard in fading dark spots and consider it safe when used for a short time.

## 3. Witch Hazel

You just might own a bottle of this skin care ingredient at home. Witch hazel is a must-have product for some women to use as an astringent, moisturizer or to treat sun damaged skin. This ingredient is totally plant-based and is ideal for ladies with oily skin to refresh, smooth and balance skin!

## 4. Retinoids

If you use skin care products for aging skin, there’s no doubt you’ve come across this common skin care ingredient! Retinoids, also known as Vitamin A, fights wrinkles, increases skin cell turnover and is even said to shrink oil glands! If you have larger pores or aging skin, look for this ingredient in your skin care!

## 5. Hyaluronic Acid

Aging or dry skin will love this next common skin care ingredient! Hyaluronic acid, which is actually a component of connective tissue, is said to hold up to 1,000 times its weight in water! It does wonders to hydrate skin, help diminish the appearance of wrinkles and sun damage. Look for this ingredients in moisturizers and mixed in with makeup!

## 6. Alpha Hydroxy Acids

AHA’s are also common skin care ingredients with acids in them that you shouldn’t shy away from! The acids in Alpha Hydroxy Acids are actually derived from fruit or milk and they do a fab job in exfoliating and revitalizing our skin! As with any use of an exfoliating product, make sure you follow up with sunscreen!

## 7. Peptides

Decreased collagen and elasticity in the skin calls for a skin care ingredient like peptides! Peptides pack a powerful punch and can reduce the look of wrinkles by plumping the skin as well as smooth out and moisturize dry or rough skin. Look for peptides in anti-aging products and even in lip products!

## 8. Silicone

Silicone is another one of the common skin care ingredients that you’ll find in many beauty products. While you might be turned off by the name, does it remind you of needles and fake boobs? Silicone in skin care helps protect the skin and even help decrease the appearance of scar tissue! Look for the good silicone in toners and moisturizers!

## 9. Glycerin

This next common skin care ingredient is frequently seen in soaps but it’s also found in many skin care products. Glycerin can moisten parched skin, help restore water levels and prevent drying in skin. Glycerin is also great for those who have eczema and can help calm itchy skin!

## 10. Jojoba Oil

Common skin care ingredients sometimes have funny or hard-to-pronounce names but I love saying jojoba oil! This is a natural oil derived from plants that is great for your scalp, skin and lips! You can use jojoba oil straight up for some uses or look for it in moisturizers. It’s easily absorbed and is ideal to use in the colder weather!

Were you aware of all of the benefits of these common skin care ingredients? I don’t always take the time to look at the ingredients in my skin care products, but I know I should and I’m definitely going to start! It’s good to be aware of the bad ingredients that are commonly found in skin care but it’s also nice to know what the good guys do too! Do you read your skin care labels?
