# 7 Things to do for Oily Skin That Cuts the Grease ...

If you’re looking for things to do for oily skin, you’ve come to the right place. There’s nothing more frustrating than looking in the mirror only to see your face shining back at you. It happens to most everyone at some point, even if you normally have dry skin. Believe me – I have epically dry skin, but I’m not exempt from oily periods too. Fortunately, you can stave off oil and even mask it when it does happen. Read on for all the help you need when it comes to things to do for oily skin.

## 1. Wash Several Times per Day

Most of us wash our skin at least once every day. At least I hope you’re all washing off your make-up before bed! However, if you have oily skin, washing it several times during the course of the day can help and is one of the easiest things to do for oily skin. I know – you may have to reapply your make-up, but at least it’ll stay in place if you clean the oil off. Try gently cleansing your face morning, noon and night. When you do that, you rinse away all the grime and dirt the oil picks up and at the same time, you get rid of that dreaded shiny look.

## 2. Drink Lots of Water

Water is good for keeping your skin hydrated and healthy, but it also helps flush toxins out of your system. Without it, those toxins are secreted through your pores, leaving you shiny and oily. Carry a water bottle with you during the day and sip often to keep your skin healthy, plumped full of moisture and not a bit oily!

## 3. Try Oil Fighting Make-up

There are several types of foundations and powders that are formulated to fight oily skin, so it pays to give them a try. They work by absorbing the oil your skin produces and also being easy on your pores to prevent acne and other blemishes. You can find them at most drugstores and department store make-up counters. Talk to your dermatologist about the best product for your skin.

## 4. Blotting Papers

I love these! You can carry them in your purse or pocket and they don’t take up much space at all. When you can feel the shine kick in, simply pull one out and blot it over the oily areas. The paper will soak up a good portion of the oil and then you can repair your look with make-up or face cream to keep the oil at bay. Blotting papers are cheap and easy to find, so they’re a handy little item to keep on hand all the time.

## 5. Moisturize

You might be wondering how in the world putting any lotion on your face can possibly fight oil. Let me clarify. Not just any lotion will do the trick, but you can’t skip moisturizing your face or you’ll end up with more problems than just the shiny skin you’re dealing with now. That’s because dry skin will just get more oily. I know – it doesn’t make sense, but all the experts say it. Look for products specially formulated for oily skin and remember that you don’t need a huge gob to get the job done.

## 6. Use a Clay Mask

Every so often, apply a clay mask to your face. It helps absorb excess oil and leaves you with glowing, radiant skin. You don’t want to do this every day, but once a week or every couple of weeks can really give you the results you crave.

## 7. Eat Right

What you eat plays a huge role in the health and appearance of your skin, so it’s definitely a good idea to pay attention to what’s on your plate. A healthy variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein and low-fat dairy is your best bet for covering the daily intake recommendations for all the vitamins and minerals that keep your skin glowing. A deficiency of vitamin B2 can also produce oilier than normal skin, so you may want to boost your intake with whole grains, beans and nuts.

Do you have oily skin? What do you do to keep it under control? Please feel free to share your tips and tricks. You can really help a girl out!
