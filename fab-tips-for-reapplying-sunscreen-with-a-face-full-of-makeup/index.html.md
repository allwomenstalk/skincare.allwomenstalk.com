# How to Reapply Sunscreen Over Makeup Like a Pro

We all know how important it is to wear sunscreen. But did you know that you should be reapplying your SPF every two to three hours? Many of us struggle with how to keep up with this when we're wearing makeup. It can feel like you have to choose between protecting your skin and keeping your makeup looking fresh.

You shouldn't have to choose between healthy skincare practices and looking your best. Here are some techniques to help you reapply sunscreen over your makeup without ruining your look. For more on building a great routine, check out our guide to [essential skincare steps](https://skincare.allwomenstalk.com/the-essential-skincare-routine-everyone-needs/).

## 1. Correctly Prep Your Skin with Sunscreen

Heidi Waldorf, director of laser and cosmetic dermatology at the [Mount Sinai Hospital](https://www.mountsinai.org/), notes that many people miss spots like the hairline, sides of the cheeks, jawline, and neck because they only apply product to the center of the face. Make sure you apply your base layer thoroughly to these often-forgotten areas.

## 2. Try a Moisturizer with Built-in SPF

If your skin is on the sensitive side, streamline your routine with a moisturizer that includes SPF. It's great for the cooler months when you need extra hydration. Just ensure you are using enough product to actually get the level of protection listed on the label. Check out some [highly rated options here](https://skincare.allwomenstalk.com/the-best-moisturizers-with-spf-for-daily-use/).

## 3. Opt for a Tinted Moisturizer with SPF

Swap out your heavy foundation for a lighter formula like a BB cream with a hint of tint. This makes touch-ups easier and keeps your skin protected throughout the day.
