# 7 Important Things to Know if You Have Sensitive Skin ...

There are several things to know if you have sensitive skin. That’s because understanding your body’s largest organ is the single best way to take the best care of it that you can. Many people suffer from the issue and in some cases it can really affect your quality of life. If you’re fed up and want things to change, check out these things to know if you have sensitive skin. You’ll be ready to love the skin you’re in with very little time and effort.

## 1. Definition

One of the best things to know if you have sensitive skin is exactly what constitutes the issue. By gaining a better understanding regarding the symptoms, you’ll know whether what plagues you really is sensitive skin or if you might have another issue. According to WebMD, dermatologists look for skin that is very dry, skin reactions and flushing skin. When those signs are present, there’s a pretty good chance that you have sensitive skin.

## 2. Diagnosis

You might have the feeling that you suffer from sensitive skin, but the only way to find out for sure is to see a dermatologist. He or she can talk with you about your symptoms and get a good look at your skin, which is a good way to get a firm diagnosis. Once you have that, you can seek out appropriate treatment methods.

## 3. Various Symptoms

The thing with sensitive skin is that affects everyone who suffers from it differently. You might experience different symptoms than a friend with sensitive skin. Likewise, the severity of your symptoms is likely to differ from someone else with the issue. According to skin experts and Women’s Health Magazine, you might experience redness, burning, itching, peeling and stinging. Bumps or pustules on your skin are other possible signs of sensitive skin.

## 4. Products Bother You

No one is going to go through their entire life without having a skin reaction to something, but if it happens to you all the time, you might have sensitive skin. Detergent, lotions, soap, body wash, perfume and hair products are all things that can exacerbate sensitive skin. If you notice skin reactions after using a certain product or if the issue is constant, talk to your dermatologist about using natural products or finding special items that won’t harm your skin.

## 5. Testing

Usually, your doctor will diagnose sensitive skin by looking at your body, but if you have allergies that contribute to the condition, there are certain tests that can help determine triggers. They are called patch tests and involve exposing your skin to possible triggers to help your doctor help you figure out what things you should be staying away from.

## 6. Choosing Products

Sometimes simply buying something that says it’s good for sensitive skin isn’t always the best choice. The best way to find a product that is easy on your skin is to find one that has very few ingredients and no added dyes of fragrances, according to WebMD. You want to avoid any type of antibacterial ingredients as well as retinoids and alcohol.

## 7. Skincare Routine

Another very important thing to consider when you have sensitive skin is your skincare routine. You’ll need to cleanse and moisturize on a daily basis to keep triggers away from your skin as well as to keep it properly hydrated. You don’t have to skip make-up, but be sure you scrutinize the ingredients before use.
