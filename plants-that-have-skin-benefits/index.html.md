# 7 Plants That Have Skin Benefits ...

When you choose skincare products, you might be overwhelmed by the number of ingredients in them, including plants that have skin benefits. If you’re anything like me, choosing products that have natural and safe ingredients is ideal. That’s why finding skincare products made with healthy plants is such a great thing. There are so many plants that have skin benefits and reading ingredients labels is the best way to find them. Check out some of the most common and you’ll be well on your way to the best skincare possible.

## 1. Aloe Vera

![Aloe Vera](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/os/is/gjphujdr_500x750.jpg)

Aloe vera tops my list of plants that have skin benefits because it’s pretty common in skincare products, including lotion. Aloe is extremely soothing and hydrating, which makes it perfect for the skin all over your body. The gel of an aloe vera plant contains a wealth of nutrients, including vitamins A, C, E, folic acid, zinc and potassium. You can even snap off the end of an aloe vera leaf and rub it directly on a burn for instant relief.

## 2. Lavender

![Lavender](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/sw/j3/j6sg0sfv_500x688.jpg)

Maybe you’ve used lavender to soothe your frazzled nerves, but experts say it has skin benefits too. Products that contain lavender work to soothe your skin. It also has antiseptic properties that make it great for cleansing. Lavender is great for people with sensitive skin because it is so soothing. You can also combine lavender oil with water to create your own skin spray.

## 3. Rose

![Rose](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/du/o6/h44s0k8p_511x796.jpg)

Not only do roses smell good, but they can also boost the health of your skin. Rose has been linked to perfectly hydrated skin and also works to soothe overworked skin. If all that wasn’t enough, experts also say that rose is great as a toner and can ease redness and irritation from breakouts. Sounds good enough for me!

## 4. Calendula

![Calendula](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ym/cz/rs42ljg6_600x760.jpg)

Calendula is a plant that can help relieve dryness and make your skin look and feel more hydrated. It also boosts blood flow, so using it on your face can boost the young and radiant appearance you crave. Calendula has also been used to treat burns, so if you run out of aloe vera, try this instead.

## 5. Tea Tree

![Tea Tree](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ma/nc/zvs3r9og_600x900.jpg)

You’ve probably heard a ton about tea tree oil. It is definitely getting its time in the spotlight recently. For good reason too, it seems. Apparently, tea tree oil is an ideal plant choice for treating acne. As we all know, adulthood doesn’t necessarily mean the disappearance of breakouts. So, next time you’re battling a case of pimples, try a skincare product that lists tea tree oil as one of its ingredients.

## 6. Witch Hazel

![Witch Hazel](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/yc/ns/oors32d1_400x600.jpg)

If you’ve ever used witch hazel pads after giving birth, you know just how soothing it is. However, that’s not the only thing you can do with it. Witch hazel is also perfect for cleansing your face. It helps clear away dirt, bacteria and debris that can lead to breakouts. It’s also great for relieving skin irritation and fighting inflammation, so keep some handy in your medicine cabinet all the time.

## 7. Neem Oil

![Neem Oil](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/uc/od/m9ha6ry9_600x800.jpg)

It doesn’t sound very plant like, but the neem tree produces this powerful oil. It’s a great choice if you suffer from chronic skin issues, such as eczema, psoriasis or recurrent breakouts. Neem oil also has loads of antioxidants, which means it supports healthy and youthful looking skin.

What products with plants do you use on your skin? It always feels great to pamper your skin and take great care of it. Will you look for any of these ingredients next time you shop for skincare products?
