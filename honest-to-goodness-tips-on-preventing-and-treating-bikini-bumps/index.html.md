# 7 Honest to Goodness Tips on Preventing and Treating Bikini Bumps ...

Preventing and treating bikini bumps are a common quandary amongst women. Although we can take steps to prevent them from happening in the first place, we rarely talk about them until we get them! If you’re tired of dealing with bikini bumps, razor bumps and ingrown hairs, let’s talk about what we can do in preventing and treating bikini bumps. Believe me, it’s easier than you think!

## 1. Witch Hazel

![Witch Hazel](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/skincare/2013/08/1_witch-hazel_599x858.jpg)

Anyone interested in preventing and treating bikini bumps should know about this inexpensive treatment! Witch hazel has lots of uses with one of them being to treat skin irritation which is extremely helpful when you’re trying to prevent or treat bikini bumps. Just grab a cotton pad, soak it with some witch hazel and apply it to the area you want to treat.

## 2. Shave Smart

![Shave Smart](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/skincare/2013/08/2_shave-smart_290x287.jpg)

Shaving your delicate bikini area takes some finesse since it’s a visible area and you want your skin to look as clear as possible. Before you pick up a razor, make sure you take a warm shower to soften the hair, exfoliate and use a thick shaving gel or cream. Avoid stretching your skin to shave and try to use the fewest number of strokes possible to avoid irritation. Pat some cool water on your skin afterward, moisturize and you’re done!

## 3. Hydrocortisone Cream

![Hydrocortisone Cream](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/skincare/2013/08/3_hydrocortisone-cream_460x273.jpg)

Hydrocortisone cream is another skin care product to familiarize yourself with when you’re shaving your bikini area. Hydrocortisone cream is typically used for a variety of skin conditions such as allergies, eczema, dermatitis and rashes. According to TLC.com, using this cream can be helpful in preventing and treating irritation or bumps after you shave.

## 4. Go with the Grain

![Go with the Grain](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/skincare/2013/08/4_go-with-the-grain_500x386.jpg)

Another shaving tip to be aware of is to always use sharp razors when shaving and shave with the grain. Of course you can always shave against the direction of hair growth but that can put you at risk for ingrown hairs. Shaving in the same direction of the hair growth also prevents irritation and should cut the hair at a better, smooth angle.

## 5. Deodorant

![Deodorant](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/skincare/2013/08/5_deodorant_261x389.jpg)

Using deodorant might be second nature to you but have you ever used it in your bikini area? That’s right ladies, you can apply some deodorant to your skin right after you shave to prevent irritation. Just use any old brand you want, just avoid using the gel type since it might stain your swimsuit or clothes. Also, using a separate stick for your underarms is recommended.

## 6. Aloe Vera

![Aloe Vera](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/skincare/2013/08/6_aloe-vera_299x295.jpg)

Aloe vera is one of those super versatile plants where there are more uses for it than you can keep count. Try applying cutting open an aloe vera leaf and applying the fleshy part or using store bought aloe vera gel. Applying aloe vera in either form can help relieve skin irritation, heal skin, moisturize and relieve itching. Give it a try!

## 7. Stop Shaving?

![Stop Shaving?](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/skincare/2013/08/7_stop-shaving_500x620.jpg)

According to WebMD, the best way to treat razor or bikini bumps is to stop shaving. For those who can’t go without shaving, they advise using a needle to release embedded hair with care. You can also use depilatories but they too can cause irritation and shouldn’t be used more than once or twice a week. You can also consider waxing the area or using an epilator.

Bikini bumps are an unsightly skin care concern, but there are lots of ways to prevent and treat them! Even if you’ve had bikini bumps in the past, there are ways you can prep and soothe your skin. Do you deal with bikini bumps? How do you treat them?
