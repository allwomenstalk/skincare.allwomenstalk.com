# 7 Signs That You Need to See a Dermatologist ...

There are many signs you need to see a dermatologist, and each of them is a good reason to make an appointment right away. Letting it go too long can leave you uncomfortable or in pain, and in some cases can lead to dangerous complications you definitely don’t want to deal with. Your dermatologist is trained to recognize a load of issues that can be treated in a variety of ways. So it never pays to ignore skin issues when there are so many doctors who can help. Check out these signs you need to see a dermatologist and get your phone out and make a call to the derm’s office.

## 1. Actinic Keratoses

This condition shows up as age spots or red, scaly patches. It’s commonly associated with the elderly, but isn’t restricted to that population. This is one of the tops signs you need to see a dermatologist because these spots are often precancerous. While every little spot on your skin isn’t in this category, it pays to have them checked out regularly so that if anything is troublesome, it can be caught early.

## 2. Growing Mole

Most moles are perfectly harmless and will never change in size and appearance. However, sometimes a mole transforms. When that happens, there is a possibility that you have some type of skin cancer. Caught early, most forms of skin cancer are treatable and you can be cancer-free afterward. Not having it checked out means the cancer can spread, which makes it much harder to treat.

## 3. Unhealing Wounds

Wounds that won’t heal are another sign that something might be wrong. Experts say that if you have a cut, scrape or blemish that won’t go away or that heals, then comes back, it’s important to get to the dermatologist right away. This can be a sign of skin cancer and it pays to have it looked at before it gets worse.

## 4. Changing Spots

No one has perfectly creamy skin without a single blotch, freckle or mole. It’s important to become familiar with yours so that if one starts looking different, you’ll know right away. If you notice color changes or a change in shape, have those spots evaluated by a dermatologist.

## 5. Unresponsive Acne

Everyone has a breakout now and then, but if you have acne that doesn’t get better with treatment, see your doctor. That way you can have the issue assessed and make changes to your treatment plan, if necessary. In some cases, over-the-counter meds don’t work well and you need a prescription that is much stronger and more effective.

## 6. Weird New Skin Issues

If you notice anything new or different on any part of your skin, you should have it checked out. You may have developed eczema or could be having an allergic reaction to something. Your dermatologist can help you get to the root of the problem so it can be treated or otherwise taken care of.

## 7. Scars

If you’ve sustained a serious injury and are left with a scar, your dermatologist can help. There are many ways you can minimize the appearance of a scar so you feel more comfortable about the way it looks. A doctor can also keep their eye on the scar to be sure it’s healing as it should.
