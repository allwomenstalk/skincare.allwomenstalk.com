# The Benefits of Using Salicylic Acid in Your Skincare Routine ...

Wondering **why you should use salicylic acid in your skincare routine**? If you are invested in your beauty routine and skincare, then I am sure that salicylic acid is definitely something that you will have heard of. The common misconception about this skincare ingredient is that it should only really be used by people who are suffering from acne, but this isn’t necessarily the case at all. The truth is the salicylic acid is something that can be used for any number of ailments related to the skin, and it seems as though everybody is starting to catch on to its various positive uses and properties. Don’t miss out on this skincare secret. Here's **why you should use salicylic acid in your skincare routine**.

## 1. Blackheads, Acne, Clogged Pores

One of the best reasons **why you should use salicylic acid in your skincare routine** is to clear pores. If you tend to struggle with any kind of congestion in the skin on your face, then, of course, salicylic acid is an essential product to use. It has the amazing ability to loosen the plugs in your pores and dissolves pesky debris that causes horrible blackheads. The anti-bacterial properties of the acid will also help with reducing the risk of inflammation and infection, calming down inflamed pimples to great effect.

## 2. Oily Skin, Large Pores

Did you know that salicylic acid is one of the few beauty ingredients that can actually slow down the production of oil in the skin? The trick to perfecting this is in finding the rough amount to use for your own skin, and working out the frequency of use to keep your skin from producing too much oil too quickly, without, of course, going the other way and drying it out completely! It can also help to make your large pores look smaller because the reason they look so big is that they are clogged with dead skin cells and oil.

## 3. Rough, Aged, Dull Skin

Salicylic acid is known to have exfoliating properties, which means that a regular regime of using the acid on your skin can transform it from being rough and dull to glowing and much younger looking. Exfoliation will lead to a reduction in the thickness of the layers of dead skin cells on your face, giving it a much brighter appearance than before.

## 4. Hyperpigmentation

Another of the acid’s great qualities is that it is a great pigmentation fighter. It has the ability to prevent melanin formation with a very low risk of irritation, which means that it is perfect for the treatment of things like correcting sun spots and age spots, as well as other more technical hyperpigmentation issues.

## 5. Safer than Glycolic Acid

One of the best reasons to use salicylic acid is that it is much safer than glycolic alternatives. Glycolic acid actually has the potential to kill live tissue, which is certainly counterproductive to helping your skin be healthier! This can happen thanks to a calcium overload, which you want to avoid at all costs.
