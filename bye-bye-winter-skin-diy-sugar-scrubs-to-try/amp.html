Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I use a sugar scrub on my skin?
Use a sugar scrub 1-3 times a week to gently exfoliate without irritating your skin.
Can sugar scrubs help with dry winter skin?
Yes, sugar scrubs help remove dead skin and lock in moisture, making them great for dry winter skin.
Are DIY sugar scrubs safe for sensitive skin?
They can be, but choose gentle ingredients like coconut oil and avoid harsh scents if you have sensitive skin.
What ingredients do I need to make a basic sugar scrub?
Typically, sugar, a moisturizing oil like coconut or olive oil, and optional scents like vanilla or lemon.
Can sugar scrubs be used on the face?
Use a finer sugar or gentler scrub for the face and always be gentle to avoid irritation.
How do I store my homemade sugar scrub?
Keep it in an airtight container at room temperature to maintain freshness and prevent clumping.
Will sugar scrubs help with cellulite?
Sugar scrubs can improve skin texture and circulation but won't eliminate cellulite completely.
Can I use sugar scrubs on my lips?
Yes! Use a gentle sugar scrub to exfoliate dry, flaky lips before applying balm.
Is it okay to use sugar scrubs during pregnancy?
Generally yes, but avoid essential oils if pregnant and consult your doctor if unsure.
How do I know if a sugar scrub is working for my skin?
Your skin should feel smoother, softer, and more hydrated after use without redness or irritation.
