# 7 Terrific Travel Skin Care Tips You Should Follow ...

If you’re planning on taking a trip in the near future, be sure you jot down some of these helpful travel skin care tips! Traveling is great, but keeping up with your skin care routine while you’re on the road can be difficult when you’re limited on time and space. Not to worry, I’ve got some valuable travel skin care tips that’ll help you look great without all the time and comforts of home!

## 1. Know the Climate

Let’s get started on this these travel skin care tips by deciding what you’re going to pack. Research the climate and weather forecast of where you’ll be traveling to beforehand to help you decide what to take along. Will you be in dry or humid weather? Prepare yourself for moisture loss, breakouts from humidity or strong winds. If you’re traveling to drier weather, you want to replenish the moisture in your skin without weighing it down with heavy creams. Go for serums and lightweight moisturizers. If you’ll be in humid weather, exfoliate your skin to get rid of dead skin cells and use a cleanser with salicylic acid.

## 2. Get Packing

Now that you know what your skin will be up against, let’s get further into packing! If you’re flying, you’ll be even more limited on what you can take with you, but that doesn’t mean you’ll have to compromise on good skin care! Think of your trip as a chance to give your skin a bit of a break from all the beauty and skin care products you normally use. You might find that your skin does better without all the products! Get down to the basics like cleanser, sunscreen and moisturizer. I like to take face oil so I don’t have to take an extra eye cream.

## 3. Downsize

This travel skin care tip is probably the most obvious, but that doesn’t make it less important than the others! No matter how far you’re going, you won’t be able to lug around your home supply of skin care products. Look into finding sample or travel size versions of your favorite skin care items. Only pack what matters because you’ll likely be able to get things like soap and lotion at your hotel, and you’ll have more room for your must-haves!

## 4. Prepare

If you’re traveling to a climate that your skin is not used to or you’re trying new skin care products, you can always prep your skin a few days ahead. Start using your new skin care products about three days before your departure date so your skin starts getting acclimated. Your skin likes routines and will look its best when you stick to the basics.

## 5. Avoid Harsh Products

Another one of the important travel tips for skin care is to avoid using any harsh products before or while you travel. You don’t want to use any products that can cause an unexpected breakout or cause irritation while you’re on the road. Avoid any harsh peels, skin resurfacing treatments or acids that can irritate or aggravate your skin.

## 6. Eat and Drink Healthy

In addition to following these travel tips for skin care, don’t forget the importance of following a healthy lifestyle whilst traveling! Exercise, healthy meals and drinking water are key to keeping your skin looking its best no matter where you are. Good skin starts from within so don’t rely only on your skin care products. Do your part to eat right, stay hydrated and get your blood flowing by engaging in some physical activity while traveling.

## 7. Be Efficient

Lastly, after you come back from your trip it might be a good idea to keep a separate stocked travel beauty bag. This way, you’ll always be prepared no matter what comes up and you’ll have all your travel necessities within your reach. Keep a list of things you used or needed on your last trip so you’ll have it all ready to go for next time, and you won’t have to start over from scratch!

I hope these travel tips for skin care were helpful for you! I can’t tell you how many times I’ve traveled and I packed too many of the wrong skin care items and I was stuck with a bunch of things I didn’t need! How do you like to pack your skin care products? Do you have any skin care tips or tricks you rely on for travel?
