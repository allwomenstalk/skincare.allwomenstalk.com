# How to Love the Skin You Have ...

Our skin is always working for us, keeping fluids in, preventing dehydration, keeping germs out, and allowing us to feel the world around us. While our skin is always working for us we tend to not give it the attention it needs which leads to various health issues. If you are feeling generally unwell it might be because you have not been taking good care of your skin. There are many ways we can show our skin the love it deserves and cure common skin conditions. Take care of your skin so your skin can take care of you like it always has.

## 1. Blackheads

No matter your age when blackheads appear, you are going to feel stressed and wonder why it seems like your skin hates you. So your first question might be: [what are blackheads](https://www.katesomerville.com/us/en/blog/what-are-blackheads.html) and what can you do about them? Blackheads are caused by excess sebum and oil being produced by the skin. Using a daily cleanser and gentle exfoliator is a great way to keep blackheads at bay. Don’t let blackheads crimp your style and make sure you take time to relax because it has been found that stress can make blackhead outbreaks worse. We all experience blackheads so remember you are not alone in your struggle.

## 2. Dry Skin

While dry skin can occur at any time, winter is a season when dry skin tends to be worse. Dry skin is especially common in the winter months because humidity levels drop, and the air is cold and dry which means the water in your skin evaporates more quickly. So what can we do about dry winter skin. The best course of action is to get yourself a [good cream or lotion](https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbes-personal-shopper/2020/12/22/9-best-dry-hand-creams/?sh=6ab59acc664d)  and make sure you are staying hydrated by drinking enough water and using a humidifier to keep the air in your home more moist. A great way to keep your skin healthy is to make sure you keep your skin hydrated. While winter can be challenging for our skin if we take a few simple steps we can keep our skin young and supple even in the dead of winter.

## 3. Reading Our Skin

Our skin provides us a lot of clues about our overall health and wellness. We know if someone looks pale or red in the face it might be a sign of an underlying health condition. It turns out that our skin can also tell us about [nutrient deficiencies](https://skincare.allwomenstalk.com/nutrient-deficiencies-you-need-to-scan-your-skin-for/) we might not know we have otherwise. For example dandruff can be caused by low zinc levels, or a lack of iron can cause your hands to appear pale. If you have a common skin issue don’t ignore it instead listen to what your skin is trying to communicate with you about your health.

## 4. Serious Skin Conditions

There are many skin conditions which may need the support of a professional. Some of these conditions include eczema, psoriasis, rosacea, or hives. If you are struggling with the health of your skin it may be time to talk to a dermatologist who can introduce you to various treatments that might help you feel more comfortable in your own skin. Do not be ashamed if your skin is struggling to keep itself healthy. Sometimes we are simply born with skin that needs a little extra TLC, and knowing what your particular skin needs help with, means you can help your skin take care of you like it is supposed to, with minimum discomfort on your part.

How are you going to take care of your skin today? Are you going to get a great moisturizer? Are you going to grab your favorite daily cleanser or gentle exfoliator? Are you going to take a moment and really listen to what your skin might be telling you about your overall health? Are you going to check in with a dermatologist? No matter who you are or what state your skin is in right now, there is always something you can do to make your skin be better able to do its job of keeping fluids inside and keeping germs outside of your wider body. So go forth and take care of your skin today, you will not regret it.
